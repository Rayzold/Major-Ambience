# Trailer Storyboard — 60s and 30s cuts

> Beat‑by‑beat script for the launch trailer. The 60s cut is the hero asset; the 30s cut is a tighter version of the same idea for paid social.
>
> All onscreen type uses **Cormorant Garamond** (display) and **Geist** (UI). Italic gold (`#e3b66a`) is the only emphasis treatment. No emoji. No sound effects metadata on screen.

### Production notes

- **Pace:** roughly 6 scene cuts in the first 30 seconds, slowing to longer holds in the second half. Mirrors the product's promise — busy when you need it, calm when you don't.
- **Music bed:** original score commissioned (or licensed‑clean royalty‑free) in **Tavern → Tension → Combat → Calm** arc. The trailer's audio *demonstrates* the product without saying so.
- **VO:** warm baritone, mid‑pace, dry recording. Not announcer. Conversational.
- **No third‑party logos.** No D&D, no Pathfinder, no Critical Role.
- **Captions baked in.** TikTok and Twitter autoplay muted.

## B‑roll (30s, no voiceover)

For broadcasters and creators who want to embed a loop in their own video.

- **0:00 – 0:08** — UI screens fly through (Library → Scenes → Soundboard → Now Playing). Smooth pan + crossfade, not hard cuts.
- **0:08 – 0:16** — Visualizers, scrubbers, pad fires. Tight macro shots of the UI.
- **0:16 – 0:24** — GM at table (hand only, no face), clicking and adjusting. Audio is the music itself, no narration.
- **0:24 – 0:30** — Slow logo settle. Hold the title card for 5 seconds.
