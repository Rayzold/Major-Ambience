## Tier 1 — Top creators (>500K subs)

**Examples:** Matt Colville, Ginny Di, Bob World Builder, the Dungeon Dudes, Critical Role network.

**Strategy:** these creators get 20+ pitches a week. Stand out by **not asking for anything**. Send the product. Maybe they mention it. Maybe they don't. The placement is worth $50–100K if it lands and your math doesn't depend on it.

### Email — first contact (Tier 1)

**Subject:** A free copy of something we built for GMs

> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm [Your Name], a [hobby/professional] GM who built an audio companion app for tabletop RPGs. It's called Major Ambience and it ships on Windows in Q3.
>
> No pitch — I'd just love to send you a free Major‑tier license (and the DM Toolkit add‑on) to play with. No expectations of coverage or even a reply.
>
> If you've ever wanted a one‑time‑purchase replacement for Syrinscape that works with your own music folder, this is that. If not, throw the license to someone on your team who runs games.
>
> Code: **[code]** — redeem at majorambience.com/redeem
>
> Sixty‑second trailer if you've got a minute: [trailer URL]
>
> Thanks for everything you do for this hobby.
>
> [Your Name]
> majorambience.com

**Send timing:** D‑14 from launch (two weeks before). Don't follow up. If they care, they'll come back.

## Tracking & follow‑up rules

| Rule | Why |
|---|---|
| **One follow‑up max per creator.** | More than that is harassment. |
| **Personalize the first line.** | Mention a specific recent piece of their work. Boilerplate dies. |
| **Send during business hours in their timezone.** | Higher reply rate. |
| **No PDF attachments.** | Inline only. Links to the press kit, never attachments. |
| **Track in a spreadsheet.** | Name, channel, audience size, contact method, status, last‑touched date, notes. |
| **Don't pitch friends.** | If you'd consider them a friend, they'll cover it without a pitch. Pitching strains the relationship. |
| **Be ready to walk away.** | A "no" or no‑reply is fine. The TTRPG community is small; don't burn bridges. |

## Banned phrases

Don't use these — they signal "PR template" and tank reply rates.

- ❌ "Reach out"
- ❌ "Circle back"
- ❌ "Synergy"
- ❌ "Game‑changer"
- ❌ "Revolutionary"
- ❌ "AI‑powered" (unless it actually is, which it isn't)
- ❌ "Best in class"
- ❌ "Disruptive"
- ❌ "Help you grow your audience"
- ❌ "Win‑win"

If you can't make the pitch in plain English without these, the pitch isn't ready.
