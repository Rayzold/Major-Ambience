## Fact sheet

### What it is

Major Ambience is a desktop app (with mobile companion coming) that turns a GM's existing music folder into a working audio studio for tabletop RPG sessions. It plays music, fires sound effects, and crossfades between scenes — all driven by a workflow built specifically for the person running the game.

### What it does

- **Auto‑categorizes** the user's music library into 10 buckets: Combat, Tavern, Exploration, Ambient, Horror, Tension, Rest, SFX, Voices, Sci‑Fi.
- **Plays scenes** — a snapshot of category, soundboard layout, fade, and volume that can be restored with one click.
- **Soundboard** — 24 pads across three pages, drag‑to‑assign, with one‑shot or loop modes.
- **SFX layer** — fire sound effects over playing music; the music bus ducks automatically.
- **Crossfade** — slider‑controlled, sample‑accurate.
- **Search and grade** — full‑text across the whole library; S/A/B/C/D/F grading drives weighted shuffle.

### Why it exists

Two trends collided. Tabletop RPG playing exploded post‑pandemic. The dominant audio tools — Syrinscape, Tabletop Audio, Battle Bards — built around their own curated catalogs, charged monthly. Most GMs already have music libraries: classical, film scores, OST collections, commissioned packs. They didn't need a streaming service; they needed software that turned their existing library into a tabletop instrument.

Major Ambience is the result.

### Who it's for

- Solo GMs running home games
- Professional GMs running paid sessions
- Streamers and actual‑play podcasters
- Game stores running in‑store events
- LARP and immersive theater organizers

### What it isn't

- Not a music service — we don't ship audio. The user brings the library.
- Not a DAW — no recording, no editing, no effects chain.
- Not a virtual tabletop — no maps, no character sheets.
- Not a subscription. Pay once, own forever.
